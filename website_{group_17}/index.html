SmartCane

This project is an smart cane designed to help visually impaired individuals navigate the world with confidence.

The SmartCane is an assistive device designed for visually impaired individuals, integrating a Raspberry Pi, microphone, speaker, and three sensorsâ€”HCSR04 ultrasonic sensor, XM125 millimeter-wave radar, and MPU6050 IMU. It provides audio alerts for steps and obstacles, adjusts notifications based on motion, and detects falls. Upon a fall, it sends alerts to family via Ntfy and uploads a 10-second audio recording to the cloud for review. A built-in voice assistant enables natural interaction while pausing sensor alerts for clarity. A web interface offers real-time sensor data and access to uploaded recordings, enhancing mobility and safety for users.

Motivation

Motivation

Navigating the world safely and independently remains a significant challenge for visually impaired individuals. Traditional mobility aids, such as basic white canes, provide limited feedback and are unable to adapt to complex, dynamic environments. These limitations increase the risk of accidents, such as missing steps, colliding with obstacles, or struggling to communicate during emergencies. The SmartCane addresses these challenges by leveraging modern technologies to enhance situational awareness and communication. By integrating ultrasonic, radar, and motion sensors with real-time audio feedback, the SmartCane provides precise and adaptive guidance. The inclusion of fall detection and a cloud-connected alert system ensures immediate support in critical situations. Additionally, its voice assistant and web interface empower users with access to advanced tools for better interaction and monitoring. This project aims to bridge the gap between assistive technology and the needs of visually impaired individuals, fostering independence, safety, and confidence in their daily lives.

System

The cane integrates a Raspberry Pi, microphone, speaker, and three sensors: the HCSR04 ultrasonic distance sensor, XM125 Pulse Coherent Radar millimeter-wave sensor (for obstacle distance measurement), and MPU6050 6-DoF Accelerometer and Gyro (also called IMU).

Architecture

Technical Components

  1. HCSR04 Ultrasonic Sensor:
    • 1. Detects changes in step height to prevent the user from tripping or missing a step.
    • 2. When the sensor detects a decrease in distance, the speaker announces "Upstairs."
    • 3. When the sensor detects an increase in distance, the speaker announces "Downstairs."
  2. XM125 Radar Sensor:
    • 1. Detects obstacles in the forward area and returns distance data.
    • 2. When obstacles enter the detection range, the speaker emits a single-frequency tone.
    • 3. The tone's frequency increases as the distance to the obstacle decreases, providing precise proximity feedback.
    • 4. Single-frequency sounds are chosen for their high sensitivity among visually impaired individuals and their distinctiveness in noisy environments.
  3. MPU6050 IMU:
    • 1. Tracks the cane's motion state to determine if the user is walking or stationary.
    • 2. Step detection using HCSR04 is only active when the cane is stationary to avoid false positives caused by the sensor's physical limitations.
    • 3. Detects falls and triggers two actions:
      • i. Sends a notification (text message) to the user's family via Ntfy.
      • ii. Uploads a 10-second audio recording of the environment to GCP, accessible via a website.
    • 4. During audio recording, XM125 and HCSR04 sound alerts are paused for clear audio capture, aiding the family in understanding the user's surroundings.
  4. Voice Assistant:
    • 1. Activated by gently rotating the cane.
    • 2. Allows users to communicate with the voice assistant directly.
    • 3. During interactions with the large language model, XM125 and HCSR04 sound alerts are paused for clear microphone input.
    • 4. Converts the user's speech to text for interaction with the language model. While awaiting the model's response, normal sensor functions remain active to ensure the user is aware of their surroundings.
    • 5. Once the response is received, it is converted to speech and played through the speaker, temporarily pausing XM125 and HCSR04 alerts to avoid overlapping sounds.

Additional Features:

  1. Web Interfaces:
    • 1. A webpage displaying real-time data from the four sensors (accelerometer, gyroscope, XM125, and HCSR04) for monitoring sensor status.
    • 2. Another webpage stores and allows playback of audio files recorded after a fall incident.
